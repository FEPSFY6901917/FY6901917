Abstract

 
        This paper highlights the different industrial methods and engineering disciplines required to create a road bike frame used at the very highest levels of competition. From finite element analysis being used to test 
        for critical structural regions in the frame and computational fluid dynamics being used to sculpt the various paraphernalia and componentry on modern cycling systems to better adapt the rider to overcome the resistive
        drag force.
